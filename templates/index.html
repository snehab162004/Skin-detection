What are Skin Diseases?

Skin diseases are conditions that affect your skin. These diseases may cause rashes, inflammation, itchiness or other skin changes. Some skin conditions may be genetic, while lifestyle factors may cause others. Skin disease treatment may include medications, creams or ointments, or lifestyle changes.

Skin Diseases

Eczema

Eczema, also known as atopic dermatitis, is a chronic skin condition characterized by itchy, inflamed, and red patches of skin.

Vitiligo

A condition where the immune system attacks melanocytes, causing patches of skin to lose pigment and become white..

Acne

A common skin condition caused by clogged hair follicles and oil glands, resulting in pimples, blackheads, and cysts.

Psoriasis

A chronic autoimmune condition that speeds up the life cycle of skin cells, leading to red, scaly patches on the skin.

Hemophilia

A Hemophilia is a genetic condition in which people lack either clotting factor VIII or IX .

Rosacea

Rosacea is a chronic skin condition that causes redness, visible blood vessels, and sometimes pimples on the face, often triggered by factors like heat, spicy foods, or stress..
